Research Project Website

LankaLens: AI-Powered Mobile Heritage Exploration Platform

An interactive digital heritage system combining mixed reality, Brahmi inscription recognition, live location tracking, and gamified projection mapping for Sri Lankan cultural heritage education.

Explore Research View Documents

Project Components

  • Mixed Reality Heritage Visualization
  • Brahmi Inscription Recognition
  • Live Location Map & Visit Verification
  • Gamified Projection Mapping Experience

Domain

Research Domain

Key research background, gaps, problems, objectives, methodology, and technologies used.

Literature Survey

Digital heritage systems use AR, MR, AI, computer vision, and interactive media to improve cultural heritage learning. Existing systems often focus on single features instead of integrated visitor experiences.

Research Gap

Many heritage applications lack real-time mobile deployment, interpretive transparency, local language support, and engaging educational interaction for Sri Lankan heritage locations.

Research Problem

Visitors often find it difficult to understand damaged monuments, ancient inscriptions, and site layouts without expert guidance or interactive learning support.

Research Objectives

  • Develop mobile-based MR heritage visualization.
  • Recognize and translate Brahmi inscriptions.
  • Track heritage exploration progress using maps and QR verification.
  • Improve learning through gamified projection mapping.

Methodology

The system follows a modular development approach. Each component is designed, implemented, tested, and then integrated into a larger heritage exploration platform. User testing is used to evaluate usability, engagement, and learning support.
